About Me


Michael Sabuni is a Tanzanian-Scottish photographer and visual storyteller whose work is shaped by a deep connection to land, culture, and wildlife. With a mixed heritage and upbringing influenced by both conservation and tradition, his work reflects a lifelong relationship with East Africa and a desire to tell meaningful stories through images. 

Michael works primarily with analogue photography, drawn to the process of shooting on film, the patience it requires and the value it adds in a time when imagery has become instant and disposable. Michaels practice moves between wildlife photography, documentary work, and cultural storytelling. 

Working with film, he makes his own darkroom prints, allowing for a tactile, hands-on relationship with his images. His work often explores the emotional and ecological relationships between people, animals, and place, reflecting a desire to tell stories that are personal, grounded, and enduring.
