Shuka & Soil marked my debut exhibition, held on July 1112, 2025 at Hackney Gallery in London.


The exhibition brought together a series of hand-printed photographs shot on 35mm film across Tanzania. The work explored the nomadic traditions of the Maasai, their deep relationship with the land, and the values of community and self-sufficiency that shape their way of life.


Displayed as large-scale darkroom prints, the show aimed to move away from digital consumption and instead offer a slower, tactile experience, one where visitors could feel the texture and weight of each moment. 

The exhibition also featured limited edition prints for sale and a short-run photography book, which sold out during the event, extending the story beyond the gallery walls.


The response far exceeded expectations, with a vibrant turnout and thoughtful conversations that made Shuka & Soil a truly memorable beginning to my journey as an exhibiting photographer.

I was fortunate to have the exhibition documented by two incredible photographers,  Ronan McKenzie and Michelle Pang, who each captured the atmosphere beautifully across the two days.

The exhibition ran over two days, with a private viewing on the opening night. I was truly blessed to host my first artist talk in conversation with Fatouma Ayeh, an art curator whose insight and perspective brought a new depth to the discussion of Shuka & Soil.

From Gallery Walls to Printed Pages

To accompany the exhibition, I created my first photography book, also titled Shuka & Soil. Designed to capture the full experience of the show in printed form, the book brings together the portraits, landscapes, and quiet moments on 35mm film that shaped the project. Each page reflects the themes of community, land, and simplicity at the heart of the Maasai way of life. 

The initial run sold out during the exhibition, far surpassing expectations, and additional copies have since been made, many of which are now also close to selling out.

Handmade Prints

Each print is carefully crafted by hand in the darkroom, making every piece truly unique and limited in its own right. 

Through this process Im able to refine every detail with intention, ensuring the final work carries both precision and the personal touch of being made directly by me.
