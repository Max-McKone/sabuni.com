About

Visual artist and photographer exploring the intersection of wildlife, culture, and humanity.

Michael Sabuni

Michael Sabuni is a visual artist and photographer based in Kenya, whose work spans wildlife photography, cultural documentation, fashion, and portraiture. With a deep connection to the African landscape and its people, his images capture moments that tell powerful stories of nature, tradition, and contemporary life.

His approach to photography blends technical precision with an intuitive understanding of light and composition. Whether working with film or digital mediums, Michael brings a distinctive aesthetic that honors both the subject and the viewer's experience.

Michael's work has been featured in exhibitions and publications internationally. His recent project "Shuka & Soil" explores the relationship between Maasai culture and the land, presented as part of the 2025 exhibition series.

Beyond personal projects, Michael collaborates with conservation organizations, documenting efforts to protect wildlife and support local communities. His "Material Cultures" series examines the intersection of traditional practices and contemporary sustainability.
